VOLEUR 

Playing Cards Game For 2 to 5 Players

After a long day of misdeeds, it's time to unwind with your familiar accomplices. Brace yourself for a wild showdown of cards — only with a worthy hand will you steal victory.

Objective

Be the first player to to score more than 50 points over several rounds.

In each round, players try to form the best poker-style combination with a hand of four cards, knowing they will then compete in a trick-taking phase where each aims to win the final trick.

Game Setup

  • Prepare a 52-cards deck ranked from 2 to Ace, and a way to keep score.

  • Shuffle the deck and deal six cards face-down to each player.

  • Players look at their hand, then discard two of their cards.

  • Afterwards, reveal the top card of the deck to determine the trump suit for the round.

Game Play

A round consist of four tricks. For the first trick, players play one card simultaneously. The Player with the highest trump card wins the trick. If no trump cards are played, the single hightest-ranked card wins the trick, regardless of suit.

The winner of a trick leads the next one, opponents must follow suit. If they cannot, they must play a trump card; otherwise, they can play any card. If no trump card is played, the highest card in the lead suit wins the trick.

Tricks are not collected, players keep their played cards face-up in front of them. Upon winning the fourth trick, which grants a scoring privilege, each player collects his four cards for scoring.

Exception

If there is no winner in the first trick, players start another trick simultaneously.
For example, if heart is trump and players simultaneously play 9♠-9♦-Q♠-Q♣

To earn points, players must have one of the following four-cards combinations in hand. If they have a basic combination, they score the points indicated. With a sneaky combination, they steal the same number of points from any opponent.

Players score in clockwise order, starting with the player to the left of the winner.

As a privilege, to determine his combination, the winner can use one card from an opponent's hand, the discard pile, or trump.

Clarifications

  • Scoring is limited to a single combination.

  • The Ace can be used as a One or follow the King, depending on the combination.

  • Players cannot split the points they steal between opponents.

  • Players cannot steal more points than their opponents have.

Victory

After scoring, all players' cards — including the trump — are discarded. The deck is shuffled, then each player receives a fresh hand of six cards to begin the next round.

Players continue to dispute rounds until one of them reaches 50 points. The player with the highest score at the end of that round is crowned Winner of Cards and Fortune.
